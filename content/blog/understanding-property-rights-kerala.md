When it comes to property and real estate in Kerala, understanding your rights and the legal framework is crucial to avoiding long, drawn-out disputes. 

Whether you are purchasing a new plot of land, dealing with an ancestral property, or facing a landlord-tenant issue, knowing the basics of property law can save you significant time and money.

## 1. Title Verification

Before purchasing any property, a thorough title verification is non-negotiable. This involves checking the chain of title (the history of ownership) for the past 30 years to ensure there are no encumbrances, pending litigations, or undisclosed heirs. In Kerala, it's also essential to verify the *Thandaper* (revenue records) and obtain an Encumbrance Certificate (EC) from the Sub-Registrar's office.

## 2. Partition Suits

Ancestral property often becomes a point of contention among legal heirs. Under the relevant personal laws (Hindu Succession Act, Indian Succession Act, or Muslim Personal Law), heirs have specific rights to the property. If the property cannot be divided amicably, any co-owner can file a **Partition Suit** in the civil court to claim their legal share.

## 3. RERA and Builder Disputes

With the implementation of the Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act (RERA), homebuyers in Kerala have stronger protections against unscrupulous builders. If a builder fails to deliver a project on time or deviates from the agreed-upon plans, buyers can approach the Kerala Real Estate Regulatory Authority (K-RERA) for redressal, including refunds with interest.

## 4. Landlord-Tenant Evictions

The Kerala Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act governs the eviction of tenants in urban areas. A landlord cannot arbitrarily evict a tenant; they must have valid grounds, such as rent arrears, bona fide need for personal use, or subletting without permission. Proper legal notices must be served before initiating eviction proceedings in the Rent Control Court.

## Conclusion

Property laws can be complex and highly technical. If you find yourself facing a property dispute or simply want to ensure a transaction is legally sound, it is highly recommended to consult with an experienced civil lawyer.
